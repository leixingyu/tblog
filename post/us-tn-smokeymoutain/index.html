A 4-day Scenic Getaway at the Great Smoky Mountains, Tennessee

We embarked on an adventurous four-day trip, starting from Columbus, Ohio. The journey began with a delightful excursion to the Cincinnati Premium Outlets where we indulged in some retail therapy, enjoying the vast array of shops and boutiques.

After a satisfying lunch at Walton Towne Center, we continued our journey toward Gatlinburg, Tennessee. On arrival, we checked into Cabin 801 Crystal Br Wy, a cozy retreat nestled in the serene landscape of Gatlinburg. To add to the experience, we dined at The Melting Pot, savoring a unique fondue dining experience, before retiring to our cabin for the night.

The following morning, we set off for the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, a majestic wilderness boasting rich biodiversity and stunning landscapes. One of our first destinations within the park was the Laurel Falls Trail, leading us to an 80-foot waterfall. The trail, named after the mountain laurel shrubs that bloom along its path, extends 2.6 miles and is the longest of four paved trails in the national park. After a leisurely lunch in Gatlinburg, we returned to our cabin for some rest.

Later, we explored downtown Gatlinburg, experiencing the vibrant atmosphere and the scenic beauty of the Ober Mountain Tramway Mall and Gatlinburg SkyPark.

Our third day began with a hearty breakfast at Crockett’s Breakfast Camp, followed by a return to the Great Smoky Mountains National Park. We embarked on a visit to Clingmans Dome, the highest point in the park and in Tennessee, offering breathtaking 360° views of the Smokies. We also hiked the Chimney Tops Trail, an experience that further connected us with the natural beauty of the park. After a flavorsome lunch at Loco Burro Fresh Mex Cantina in Gatlinburg, we enjoyed a relaxing session in the hot tub back at our cabin.

In the evening, we immersed ourselves in the local cuisine of Gatlinburg, enjoying a delightful dinner in the town.

Our final day began with a departure from the cabin and a visit to Smoky Mountain Knife Works, an intriguing destination for outdoor enthusiasts and knife collectors. Our journey concluded with a drive back, filled with memories of scenic landscapes, cultural experiences, and the tranquil beauty of the Great Smoky Mountains.

Throughout our trip, we were constantly amazed by the natural splendor of the region, from the serene waterfalls to the panoramic views atop Clingmans Dome. The Great Smoky Mountains National Park, with its rich history, diverse ecology, and enchanting landscapes, offered us an unforgettable experience, a blend of adventure, relaxation, and exploration.
