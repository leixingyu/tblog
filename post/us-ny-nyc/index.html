Day 1

Our journey began at LaGuardia Airport, bustling and lively as always. From there, we navigated the subway, hopping on at 5th Avenue/53rd Street, en route to one of New York’s most iconic landmarks: the Metropolitan Museum of Art in Central Park. The subway ride itself was an experience, a mix of locals and tourists, all absorbed in the rhythm of the city.

Upon arriving, our appetites led us to a nearby food truck serving Greek cuisine. The aroma of gyros and souvlaki filled the air as we indulged in a delightful lunch under the city’s towering trees.

Our stroll commenced from Central Park, weaving through the scenic paths down 5th and 6th Avenues towards Times Square. The transition from the park’s natural tranquility to the bustling city streets was seamless yet striking.

We continued our walk down to the Theater District, absorbing the vibrant energy that makes this area so famous. Our hotel, the Crowne Plaza HY36 in Midtown Manhattan, welcomed us with its sleek design and modern amenities. That evening, we dined at Cafe Salmagundi, a splendid restaurant where I had the best meal in years. The tender pork and duck confit were culinary masterpieces.

Day 2

The next day, we walked to The Edge at Hudson Yards for a breathtaking sky view of New York. The building, with its vast shopping areas, offered a greater view than the Empire State Building, thanks to its see-through glass walls. Our visit to the Vessel followed, where we waited about 30 minutes to explore its unique design.

Lunch was at Milos, close by. We indulged in luxury, savoring lobster, fried zucchini, and oysters - a pricey but worth-it experience.

After a rejuvenating nap back at the hotel, we set off for the 9/11 site. We admired the One World Trade Center from afar and browsed through various malls, including the Oculus. In the afternoon, our walk continued down Broadway to Wall Street, where we saw the famous Charging Bull. Arriving in Lower Manhattan, we viewed the boat rides to the Statue of Liberty from City Hall Park.

Our route then took us back towards NYU, where Washington Square Park and its iconic arch awaited. Dinner was at Soothr, a delightful end to our day.

Day 3

The following morning, we took the subway to Columbia University. The visitor center and bookstore were our first stops, though we found the staff somewhat rude. The campus, small and beautiful, was a contrast to the bustling city. We returned via subway, had lunch at Uluh (a modern Sichuan restraunt), and revisited the NYU bookstore for souvenirs we had missed the day before.

In the afternoon, we explored Chelsea Market and walked the High Line, savoring the unique blend of urban and natural beauty. Our visit to the newly built Little Island was a highlight, showcasing innovative design amidst the Hudson River.

Our day concluded in the SoHo district, with dinner at Piccola Cucina Osteria Siciliana - a perfect blend of ambiance and exquisite Italian cuisine, capping off another memorable day in the city that never sleeps.
