[Review] Fisherman's Landing - I vomited twice so you don't have to

2023-10-08
2023-11-12
review

I threw up twice

TLDR tips:

  • Expect to get sea sick (even with motion sickness pill), so don’t eat much before the trip
  • Share fish bag with your partner to save $2
  • Wear jacket! cold and windy on the sea

Time Stamps:

  • 12:00 PM: (Optional) Lunch at Downtown
  • 1:00 PM: Boat departures
  • 2:20 PM: First Fishing Spot
  • 4:00 PM: Second Fishing Spot
  • 5:30 PM: Boat begins to return
  • 6:30 PM: Arrival at dock

Cost:

  • Boat Ticket: $70
  • (Optional) Supplies:
    • Motion Sickness Medicine: $5
    • Sun Screen: $7
    • Fish Bag: $2
    • Fish Raffle: $3
  • Equipment Rental: $22
  • Fishing License: $20
  • (Optional) Fish Cleaning: $1-2 per fish
  • Parking: $5
  • Total: $110-$130

For those venturing into ocean fishing for the first time, I’m here to share my experience and insights.

I boarded the “Dolphin,” hopeful that we might spot dolphins or even whales during our journey Since I know there’s some trips on the coast that offers this. I was wrong because I only get to see a lot of seagulls…

Sundays offer a special treat where one adult can bring a child on board for free. This day, the boat was bustling with children excited to cast their lines. Many seasoned fishermen joined us, their confidence and knowledge slightly intimidating for a first-timer like me.

Check-In and Preparations

The check-in process at the office was hassle-free. We obtained fishing licenses and rented our gear from the counter, with ample parking nearby. The boat’s boarding process was efficient, and the staff assisted in assigning bags for storing our catches. It’s cost-effective to share a bag with a partner, as each bag costs $2, and there’s a $3 raffle for the person who lands the largest fish.

Setting Sail and Baiting

Shortly after departure, the crew prepared live fish from the onboard tank for use as bait, alongside other options like squid strips. After an hour at sea, we received a briefings on how to fish: we were to drop the hook, attached to a heavy anchor, down to the ocean floor, then raise it slightly above the seabed. This is where the fish congregate to bite, and we were advised that the area behind the boat experiences less turbulence.

Fishing Begins

After about 1.5 hours, the boat finally anchored, and everyone was eager to cast their lines. No specific spots were assigned; people could choose any available space. Learning to operate the fishing equipment took a couple of tries, but the real challenge was sensing the bites with the boat’s constant movement.

I caught my first mackerel in under 10 minutes, followed by another. However, we started to feel queasy (even though we had taken motion sickness pills). We were far from the coast, with moderate waves, and the temperature dropped considerably, shifting from sunny to windy. The cold wind, turbulence, and motion sickness made it hard to keep fishing. I took a break and headed to the restroom to throw up.

Feeling better, I resumed fishing and caught my first sculpin, a peculiar-looking, venomous fish (they had to remove its spines before unhooking it). Unfortunately, I fell ill once more and vomited again.

Time passed quickly, but due to the time I spent resting, I estimated about 1.5 hours of actual fishing. The boat repositioned once mid-way. Those prone to motion sickness likely had even less fishing time.

The boat started heading back around 5:30 PM, and the temperature continued to drop with the wind becoming harsh. I was weak, feeling the effects of throwing up and missing my dinner routine. The journey back left me cold and miserable.

In the end, the crew helped clean and fillet our catch: we had 5 mackerels and 2 sculpins. We cooked them, but they didn’t taste different from store-bought fish. Overall, the experience was quite expensive at $300 for just a couple of fish.

Despite my motion sickness, I believe we would have enjoyed it more without it. A few other first-timers also struggled with seasickness, so it’s something to keep in mind. Overall, it was a unique experience, but I gave it a one-star rating due to the physical discomfort I endured.
