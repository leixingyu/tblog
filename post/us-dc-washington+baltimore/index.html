Although I’ve been to Washington D.C, it has been quite a while, thus I’m revisiting it again. This is also during the time of the National Day holiday, so I thought it would be perfect for a Washington D.C trip.

National Park

Important: Closure on July 4th due to firework, that includes everything west of 17th st, which includes the Lincoln Memorial and Reflecting Pool; The Washington Monument, being the center-piece of the firework is also closed down. However, visiting on July 3rd or 5th is a great alternative.

You can easily spend half a day in a single museum, especially if you’re with kids. For the larger and more famous museums, two hours is generally sufficient, and for other sites, half to one hour.

Here’s an example route from my experience:

  • National Museum of Natural History (2 hr)
  • National Gallery of Art (1 hr)
  • National Air and Space Museum (1 hr)
  • United States Botanic Garden (0.5 hr)

  • Smithsonian Castle (0.5 hr)

  • The White House (0.5 hr)

    • The Ellipse
  • Washington Monument (1 hr)
    • World War II Memorial and Reflecting Pool (1 hr)
  • Lincoln Memorial (1 hr)
    • Korean War Veterans Memorial

During my visit on July 4th, I could only catch a glimpse of Capitol Hill, the Smithsonian Castle, and the White House, primarily due to Covid restrictions that had temporarily closed down all the museums. It was a bit disappointing, to say the least.

As I mentioned, you could easily spend more time at each museum, and there are many others within the National Park and the surrounding streets.

Firework

For watching the fireworks, there are plenty of spots to sit; just make sure to arrive 30 minutes in advance and do it on foot. Expect large crowds and some traffic congestion.

The fireworks display lasts around 10-15 minutes, which can be a bit underwhelming, especially if you’re expecting music accompaniment like at Disneyland.

Chinatown

Chinatown is within a 15-minute walk from the National Park and is accessible via D.C.’s metro system. It’s not only a great dining district but also home to several nearby hotels.

I stayed at the Fairfield Inn, just across the street from Chinatown. However, I wouldn’t recommend this hotel as it feels a bit dated.

Baltimore

Baltimore, home to the famous Johns Hopkins University, is just an hour’s drive away from Washington, D.C. I’d recommend a short visit during the National Park’s closure.

Baltimore is generally clean and safe during the day, and I particularly enjoyed the dockside area. There are also plenty of parking garages available.

Thames Street Oyster House

After strolling around downtown and the dock region, we had an amazing dining experience at the Thames Street Oyster House, where we indulged in a variety of delicious raw oyster selections.

Leesburg Premium Outlets

Another option to fill your day is a visit to the Leesburg Premium Outlets, which is also about an hour away from D.C. Here, you’ll find all the popular brands you’d expect from an outlet store, including Armani and Burberry.
